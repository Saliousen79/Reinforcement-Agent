Reinforcement Learning Algorithm: PPO

We use Proximal Policy Optimization (PPO), a state-of-the-art policy gradient method that balances exploration and exploitation. PPO is known for its stability and sample efficiency in multi-agent environments.

stable-baselines3 PPO | 100,000+ timesteps | Parallel environment training

Reward Function Design

The core of learning lies in the reward signal. Our custom reward function encourages:

  • +100 Capturing the enemy flag
  • +10 Moving towards enemy flag
  • +5 Defending own flag area
  • +15 Successfully stunning an enemy
  • -1 Time penalty (encourages faster gameplay)

Training Architecture

PettingZoo multi-agent environment | Shared policy network | Vector observation space (grid-based vision, agent states, flag positions)

Phase 1: Random Chaos (Episode 0)

Agents wander aimlessly, ignoring flags and teammates. No strategic behavior emerges.

Phase 2: Tactical Play (Episode 10,000+)

Agents coordinate attacks, defend strategically, and use walls for cover. Clear role division emerges.

Zentrale Erkenntnisse

Emergente Verhaltensweisen aus dem Training

1. Pathfinding & Navigation

Agents learned to navigate around walls efficiently, discovering optimal routes to enemy territory without explicit pathfinding algorithms.

2. Role Allocation

Without being programmed to do so, agents naturally split into offensive and defensive roles, with some protecting the home base while others attack.

3. Combat Tactics

Agents developed timing strategies for using the stun ability, learning to coordinate attacks and protect flag carriers.
